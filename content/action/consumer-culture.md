## Objectives
{{< gridlist >}}
### Buy Less, Buy Better
Buy less, choose quality, and repair what you can. Resist the system’s push for endless consumption.
<--->
### Resist Lifestyle Branding
Don’t let brands define your worth. Ask: “Who profits if I buy this?”
<--->
### Make, Share, and Swap
Make, share, and swap instead of buying new. Join repair cafés or buy-nothing groups.
<--->
### Build Self-Reliance
Build self-reliance and community skills to break free from consumer culture.
{{< /gridlist >}}
## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Buy less, buy better
Choose long-lasting products from companies like Patagonia Worn Wear, Buy Me Once, or Repair-friendly brands like Fairphone and iFixit-supported products.
<--->
### Repair what you own
Use iFixit guides or attend a Repair Café to extend the life of clothing, electronics, and appliances.
<--->
### Resist lifestyle branding
Pause before purchasing and ask: “Who profits from this?” Use resources like The Story of Stuff Project to understand the hidden costs of consumerism.
<--->
### Swap and share locally
Join Buy Nothing groups, Freecycle, or local swap events to reduce new purchases.
<--->
### Practice making instead of buying
Learn DIY skills via platforms like Instructables, Skillshare, or local maker spaces.
<--->
### Build self-reliance
Grow food, mend clothes, or learn basic trade skills. Connect through community workshops or permaculture networks.
<--->
### Challenge fast fashion
Choose thrift shops, clothing swaps, or ethical brands listed in the Good On You directory instead of fueling disposable fashion.
<--->
### Support degrowth movements
Engage with networks like the Post-Growth Institute or Center for the Advancement of the Steady State Economy (CASSE) that advocate for alternatives to endless consumption.
{{< /gridlist >}}
