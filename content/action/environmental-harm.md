## Objectives
{{< gridlist >}}
### Repair, Reuse, Share
Repair, reuse, and share devices instead of buying new. Support right-to-repair laws and companies that build things to last.
<--->
### Choose Longevity
Choose products designed for longevity, not landfill. Recycle or donate electronics responsibly. Keep e-waste out of landfills.
<--->
### Demand Accountability
Demand tech companies take responsibility for their environmental impact. Challenge planned obsolescence and fast tech culture in your community.
<--->
### Support Sustainable Policy
Support policies and organizations that promote sustainability and environmental justice in your community.
{{< /gridlist >}}
## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Repair instead of replace
Use DIY resources like iFixit (repair guides + parts) and support the Right to Repair movement to extend the life of your devices.
<--->
### Buy refurbished and secondhand
Choose certified refurbished tech from platforms like Back Market, Swappa, or local repair shops instead of new devices.
<--->
### Donate or recycle responsibly
Drop off old electronics at certified e-Stewards or R2 recyclers, or donate to groups like Computers with Causes.
<--->
### Choose durable products
Support companies with long-lasting, repairable devices like Framework laptops, Fairphone, or Purism (modular, sustainable tech).
<--->
### Challenge planned obsolescence
Join campaigns like the Restart Project or Repair Café that fight fast-tech throwaway culture.
<--->
### Reduce e-waste at home
Share or lend devices through local community groups, libraries of things, or platforms like Freecycle.
<--->
### Support environmental organizations
Back groups like Greenpeace, 350.org, or Environmental Working Group that push tech companies toward accountability.
<--->
### Advocate for sustainable policy
Support local and national legislation (like the EU’s Circular Economy Action Plan or U.S. Right to Repair bills) that promote repairability and environmental justice.
{{< /gridlist >}}
