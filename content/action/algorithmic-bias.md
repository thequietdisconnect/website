## Objectives
{{< gridlist >}}
### Learn and Support
Learn how algorithms impact decisions in finance, jobs, and justice. Support efforts to audit and regulate biased algorithms.
<--->
### Advocate for Transparency
Advocate for transparency in automated decision-making. Educate others about the risks of algorithmic discrimination.
<--->
### Demand Accountability
Demand accountability from companies using AI and automation.
<--->
### Promote Inclusive Design
Encourage the development and use of algorithms that are inclusive, fair, and regularly reviewed for bias.
{{< /gridlist >}}
## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Learn how algorithms shape lives
Read reports like ProPublica’s study on predictive policing or MIT’s Gender Shades project exposing facial recognition bias.
<--->
### Take a free AI ethics course
Try Elements of AI or Data Ethics, AI, and Responsible Innovation (University of Edinburgh, free online).
<--->
### Support watchdog groups
Back the Algorithmic Justice League, Data & Society, or AI Now Institute, which audit and challenge biased systems.
<--->
### Demand algorithmic transparency
When interacting with schools, employers, or agencies, ask if decisions involve automated systems and request explanations.
<--->
### Push for independent audits
Support initiatives like the AI Audit Toolkit or laws requiring regular bias audits for companies deploying AI.
<--->
### Promote inclusive datasets
Highlight and share projects like Google’s skin-tone scale research or Inclusive Images dataset to improve representation.
<--->
### Share fairness tools
Encourage developers and organizations to use open-source toolkits like Aequitas, Fairlearn, or AI Fairness 360 for bias testing.
<--->
### Advocate for stronger policy
Support regulations like the Algorithmic Accountability Act (U.S.) or the EU AI Act that require transparency and fairness in AI.
{{< /gridlist >}}
