## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Support housing justice organizations
Back groups like the National Low Income Housing Coalition, Right to the City Alliance, or your local tenants’ union.
<--->
### Challenge real estate speculation
Join campaigns that fight corporate landlords and short-term rental takeovers, such as Anti-Eviction Mapping Project or local zoning reform efforts.
<--->
### Volunteer with frontline groups
Donate time or money to shelters, Habitat for Humanity, or grassroots groups serving the unhoused in your area.
<--->
### Educate and spread awareness
Host community teach-ins using resources from Housing Justice National Platform or share reports like the UN’s Housing as a Human Right framework.
<--->
### Advocate for tenant protections
Lobby for rent control, just-cause eviction laws, and eviction defense. Join networks like Tenants Together to strengthen protections.
<--->
### Support community land trusts
Get involved with land trusts like the Champlain Housing Trust or explore starting one in your city to keep housing permanently affordable.
<--->
### Promote cooperative housing models
Explore and support housing co-ops, mutual housing associations, or shared ownership models as alternatives to speculative markets.
<--->
### Push for policy change
Support legislation at local and national levels for affordable housing development, eviction moratoriums, and homelessness prevention programs.
{{< /gridlist >}}
