## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Diversify your news diet
Use tools like AllSides or Ground News to compare how stories are covered across the political spectrum.
<--->
### Support independent journalism
Subscribe to or donate to outlets like ProPublica, Democracy Now!, The Intercept, or your local nonprofit newsroom.
<--->
### Use nonprofit news apps
Try platforms like NewsGuard (credibility ratings) or News360 (customizable feeds without outrage bias).
<--->
### Fact-check before sharing
Verify stories with resources like Snopes, PolitiFact, or the Poynter Instituteâ€™s International Fact-Checking Network.
<--->
### Avoid clickbait traps
Install extensions like News Feed Eradicator or DeArrow to cut out sensationalist headlines and thumbnails.
<--->
### Take breaks from outrage media
Schedule "media fasts" or practice mindful consumption with apps like Freedom that block sites during focus time.
<--->
### Promote media literacy
Use free curricula from Media Literacy Now or The News Literacy Project to teach critical thinking in your community.
<--->
### Share credible alternatives
When you correct misinformation, also share trusted sources. Model how to shift the narrative without fueling outrage.
{{< /gridlist >}}
