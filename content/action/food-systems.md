## Objectives
{{< gridlist >}}
### Choose Whole Foods
Choose whole, minimally processed foods when possible. Support local farmers, co-ops, and food justice initiatives.
<--->
### Grow and Learn
Grow your own food or join a community garden. Learn about the origins and impacts of your food.
<--->
### Advocate for Change
Advocate for sustainable and equitable food policies.
<--->
### Reduce Food Waste
Plan meals, store food properly, and compost to reduce food waste and environmental impact.
{{< /gridlist >}}
## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Support local growers
Shop at farmers’ markets, join a CSA (Community Supported Agriculture), or use apps like LocalHarvest to find local farms and co-ops.
<--->
### Choose food justice initiatives
Back groups like Black Urban Growers, La Via Campesina, or your local food justice network that fight for equitable access to healthy food.
<--->
### Grow your own food
Start small with windowsill herbs, a balcony planter, or join a community garden via American Community Gardening Association directories.
<--->
### Learn where food comes from
Watch documentaries like Kiss the Ground or use resources from the FoodPrint project to understand the impact of industrial agriculture.
<--->
### Advocate for sustainable food policy
Support legislation like the Farm Bill reform in the U.S. or local initiatives for regenerative agriculture and urban farming.
<--->
### Reduce food waste
Use apps like Too Good To Go or Olio to save surplus food, and start composting with guides from ShareWaste.
<--->
### Cook and share meals
Build community resilience by hosting potlucks, food swaps, or joining a Buy Nothing group to redistribute surplus.
<--->
### Support ethical certification
Choose products with credible certifications like Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, or Regenerative Organic when local isn’t an option.
{{< /gridlist >}}
