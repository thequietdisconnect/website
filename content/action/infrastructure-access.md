## Objectives
{{< gridlist >}}
### Use and Support Public Goods
Use and support public transit, libraries, and broadband initiatives. Advocate for funding and expansion of public infrastructure.
<--->
### Challenge Privatization
Challenge privatization and underfunding of essential services. Volunteer or donate to organizations improving access.
<--->
### Educate and Advocate
Educate others about the importance of shared infrastructure.
<--->
### Build Community Projects
Help build or participate in community projects that expand access to essential services for all.
{{< /gridlist >}}
## What You Can Do
{{< gridlist >}}
### Support and use public goods
Ride public transit, borrow from libraries, and use community broadband projects to keep them funded and relevant.
<--->
### Back funding campaigns
Join local campaigns for public transit bonds, library funding levies, or municipal broadband expansions in your community.
<--->
### Challenge privatization
Support groups like In the Public Interest that track and fight the privatization of essential services.
<--->
### Volunteer locally
Donate time or money to Friends of the Library groups, transit rider coalitions, or organizations expanding public access.
<--->
### Educate your community
Host discussions or share resources from groups like the Public Services International (PSI) about why shared infrastructure matters.
<--->
### Advocate for open access
Push for free public Wi-Fi, universal broadband, and accessible public spaces through petitions and town hall advocacy.
<--->
### Build community infrastructure
Help start tool libraries, community fridges, or mutual aid broadband sharing projects in your neighborhood.
<--->
### Support policy reform
Back legislation that strengthens funding for public goods, especially local city council measures.
{{< /gridlist >}}
